# Legal Sources for Speed Limit Data

## Primary Legislation

### Traffic Act, Chapter 403 — Section 42

Establishes default speed limits:
- **50 km/h** — Within boundaries of any trading centre, township, municipality, or city
- **30 km/h** — Within boundaries of schools (nursery, primary, secondary), children's playgrounds, and health facilities

### Traffic (Speed Limits) Rules — Legal Notice 62 of 1975

Amended by LN 309/1986, LN 139/2011, LN 221/2023.

Maximum speeds by vehicle class on open highways (outside built-up areas):

| Class | Vehicle Type | Dual Carriageway | Single Carriageway |
|-------|-------------|------------------|--------------------|
| 1(a) | Motorcycles, motor-cars (excl. PSVs) | 110 km/h | 100 km/h |
| 1(b) | Commercial vehicles, matatus, PSVs | 80 km/h | 80 km/h |
| 1(c) | Articulated vehicles | 80 km/h | 80 km/h |
| 2 | Motor vehicles drawing one trailer | 65 km/h | 65 km/h |
| 3 | Vehicles with non-pneumatic tires | 30 km/h | 30 km/h |

## Specific Road Directives

### Nairobi Expressway — 80 km/h

Set by Moja Expressway operator and NTSA directive (not the dual carriageway default of 110 km/h) due to tight curves, elevation changes, and safety barriers.

## 2026 Fine Structure (NTSA Instant Fines)

| Excess Over Limit | Fine |
|-------------------|------|
| 1–5 km/h | Warning |
| 6–10 km/h | KES 500 |
| 11–15 km/h | KES 3,000 |
| 16–20 km/h | KES 10,000 |
| 20+ km/h | Court appearance |
